## Case Study: UPI QR Code Scam

Definition of the Crime UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a deceptive practice where cybercriminals exploit digital payment systems to steal money. A common variation is the QR Code Scam. This involves tricking a victim into scanning a malicious QR code under the false pretense of "receiving" a payment, whereas scanning and entering a PIN actually "authorizes" a debit from the victim's account.
Step-by-Step Process
1.	The Hook: A fraudster contacts the victim through an online marketplace (like OLX) or social media, pretending to be a buyer for an item the victim is selling.
2.	The Deception: The criminal claims they are paying from a "business account" that requires a QR code scan to "verify" the transaction. They send a QR code via WhatsApp.
3.	The Interaction: The victim, pressured by the fraudster's sense of urgency, scans the code using their UPI app (PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm). The app then prompts them to enter their secret UPI PIN.
4.	The Theft: As soon as the victim enters the PIN, they authorize a transfer out of their account. The money is instantly debited and moved to a series of untraceable "mule" accounts.

**Target Audience:**  The primary targets are often university students and young adults who are comfortable with digital technology but may not realize that a PIN is never required to receive money.

**Consequences:** The immediate impact is financial loss, which can be significant for a student. Beyond the money, victims experience psychological stress, a loss of trust in digital banking, and the complex burden of filing a formal complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.

## Scenario: The Instagram Sneaker Sale

Priya, a college student, posts on Instagram that she is selling her designer sneakers for ₹8,000. A potential buyer comments, expressing interest and asking her to DM.

Once in DMs, the buyer explains that he is using a "business UPI account" and claims that to complete the transaction, Priya must scan a QR code to "authenticate her merchant profile." He sends her a QR code on Instagram, saying, "Just scan this with PhonePe, enter your PIN, and the money will be credited instantly."

Feeling the pressure to close the sale and trusting the buyer's confident tone, Priya opens her app, scans the code, and enters her UPI PIN. Instead of receiving ₹8,000, she receives a debit alert for ₹8,000. The scammer immediately blocks her and deactivates his account.

Priya later learns that scanning a QR code and entering her PIN was an authorization to send money—not receive it. As a student, the financial loss leaves her struggling to pay her tuition installment, and she must now navigate filing a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.
